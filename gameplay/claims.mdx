You've spent hours building your base, stockpiling resources, and carefully designing your home — the last thing you want is for someone to walk in and tear it apart. That's exactly why The Grind MC has a robust land claiming system. By claiming land, you make it entirely your own: only you (and players you explicitly trust) can break blocks, open chests, or interact with anything inside your claim. It's your first line of defense against griefing, and every player should set one up as soon as possible.

## Getting Started with Claims

Before you start claiming land, here's what you need to know about how claim blocks work:

- **New players receive 1,000 claim blocks** by default when they first join the server. That's enough to protect a solid starter base.
- **Earn more claim blocks passively** — you gain **1 claim block per hour** spent online. The more you play, the more land you can protect.
- **Donor ranks receive bonus claim blocks** on top of the standard passive gain. Check out the store if you want to supercharge your claiming power.

You can check how many claim blocks you have at any time with `/claimlist`.

## Creating a Claim

<Steps>
  <Step title="Get a Golden Shovel">
    The Golden Shovel is your claiming tool. Craft one using two sticks and two gold ingots, or grab one for free from `/kit starter` if you're just starting out.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Right-Click the First Corner">
    Hold your Golden Shovel and right-click the ground at one corner of the area you want to claim. You'll see a confirmation message and a glowing block marker appear.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Right-Click the Opposite Corner">
    Walk to the diagonally opposite corner of your desired area and right-click the ground again. This sets the second corner and defines the full rectangular area of your claim.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Your Claim is Created!">
    The claim is instantly active. You'll see glowstone and gold block markers appear at the corners of your claim so you can visualize its boundaries. Everything inside is now protected — only you can modify it by default.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Info>
  You can visualize claim boundaries at any time by right-clicking the ground with a **stick**. Gold and glowstone blocks will appear along the edges of any nearby claims so you always know exactly where your protection begins and ends.
</Info>

## Managing Your Claim

Once your claim is set up, a handful of commands let you fine-tune exactly who can do what inside your land.

| Command | What It Does |
|---------|-------------|
| `/claimlist` | View all your active claims and their block counts |
| `/abandonclaim` | Remove the claim you're standing in (your blocks are refunded) |
| `/trust [player]` | Give a player full build and access permissions in your claim |
| `/containertrust [player]` | Allow a player to open chests, furnaces, and doors — but not break blocks |
| `/accesstrust [player]` | Allow a player to use buttons, levers, and pressure plates only |
| `/untrust [player]` | Remove all permissions from a specific player |
| `/claiminfo` | View detailed info about the claim you're currently standing in |

Use `/untrust all` to strip permissions from every trusted player in your claim at once — handy if you need a quick lockdown.

## Subdividing Claims

Got a large claim that you want to share with different players in different sections? Subdividing is the answer.

Type `/subdivideclaims` to enter subdivision mode. Then use the same Golden Shovel corner-clicking process to draw smaller sub-claims within your main claim area. Each sub-claim can have its own trust settings, completely independent of the parent claim. When you're done, type `/basicclaims` to return to normal claiming mode.

This is perfect for:
- **Renting out** plots to other players
- **Sharing a farm** while keeping your base private
- **Zoning your build** into public and private areas

---

<Warning>
  **Building outside a claim leaves you unprotected.** Any blocks, chests, or items placed outside a claimed area can be broken or stolen by any player. If you're expanding your base, always extend your claim to cover new areas before you start building. The `/abandonclaim` command only removes the claim you're standing in — make sure you're standing in the right one before using it, as it cannot be undone.
</Warning>

<Tip>
  **Claim smart, not big.** It might be tempting to claim as much land as possible, but claim blocks are a limited resource. Focus your claims on areas you're actively building and using. Claiming huge empty areas early on will leave you without blocks to protect future builds. You can always expand your claim later as you earn more blocks through playtime.
</Tip>
